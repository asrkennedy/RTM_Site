About RTM

Risk Terrain Modeling, or RTM, was invented by Les Kennedy and Joel Caplan at Rutgers University and has been developed in collaboration with Eric Piza . RTM is an approach to spatial risk analysis that utilizes a geographic information system to attribute qualities of the real world to places on a digitized map. It operationalizes the spatial influences of features of a landscape and models how they co-locate to create unique behavior settings for crime or other hazardous events. Risk terrain maps "paint a picture" to show where conditions are conducive for events to occur in the future. RTM offers a statistically valid way to articulate and communicate criminogenic and vulnerable areas at the micro-level.
