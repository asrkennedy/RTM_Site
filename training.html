What does the cost include?

This is a very comprehensive workshop. It starts at the introductory level and continues through intermediate/advanced. The self-guided content allows you to learn at your own pace-with readings, videos, software demonstrations and PowerPoint presentations.

Registration cost includes all course books, software (1-year ArcGIS license), the RTMDx Utility (Professional Version, single user license) and other materials. Also included is InfoGroup data for a jurisdiction of your choice (up to 1000 address-level records in the US or Canada) so you can have a customized analysis experience studying risk factors of your local environment.

Optional independent study projects will only be required of students who want a Certificate of Completion. Each student may produce an "industry standard" report demonstrating their application of risk terrain modeling methods to a topic of interest. For example, projects may be in the form of a PowerPoint presentation, crime report/bulletin, research brief, journal article, etc. Projects will be reviewed by associates at the Rutgers Center on Public Security (RCPS). Those that demonstrate a basic level of proficiency in RTM methods will earn a Certificate of Completion.

Who should attend?

Crime analysts, intelligence analysts, security professionals, researchers, criminal justice students, and GIS users interested in spatial modeling and risk assessment.

What is the time commitment?

Each student is free to commit as much time to this webinar as they desire and are able. The video/audio demonstrations in the Modules amount to about 8 hours, combined. Plus there is recommended readings provided, which would take additional time. And, if you wanted to do the optional independent study to get a certificate of completion, then that could take varying amounts of time depending on what you do. In order to get the most out of this webinar, people should invest about 20-30 hours over the entire course (some people will devote more or less depending on skill levels).

What is the required software?

This course is currently designed around both ArcGIS versions 9.3 and 10. All participants will receive an extended trial copy of ArcGIS 10.1 for installation on personal computers. All participants will receive a single user "Professional" license of the RTMDx Utility. The Professional version outputs GeoTiff maps.

To view the self-guided video presentation and other course content, you will need a broadband internet connection and the following software that can be downloaded for FREE: Microsoft Silverlight, Java, Adobe Shockwave, Adobe PDF Reader.

Are there any prerequisites?

Basic computer skills and knowledge of ArcGIS are highly recommended.
